Project Goal

Measure and compare volatility behavior across five Indian sector indices, then evaluate how well EGARCH captures persistence, asymmetry, and short-horizon risk during normal and crisis regimes.

NIFTY BANK NIFTY IT NIFTY PHARMA NIFTY FMCG NIFTY AUTO

Result Storyline

  1. Converted daily closes to log returns and built monthly return series.
  2. Tested seasonality and stationarity (Friedman, ADF, PP, KPSS).
  3. Benchmarked ARIMA-family mean models and residual adequacy.
  4. Compared GARCH(1,1) and EGARCH(1,1) by BIC across sectors.
  5. Evaluated crisis-period volatility behavior and cross-sector sensitivity.
  6. Scored rolling one-step-ahead EGARCH forecasts (RMSE, MAE, MAPE).

Results at a Glance

Seasonality Evidence

Month plots, spectra, and Friedman tests check if monthly return behavior is systematic.

Stationarity Checks

ADF, PP, and KPSS outputs validate assumptions before model fitting.

Model Selection

BIC tables compare candidate ARIMA and volatility models parsimoniously.

Residual Diagnostics

Ljung-Box and residual plots test for remaining serial structure.

Crisis Sensitivity

Cross-sector volatility behavior is profiled specifically during stress windows.

Forecast Quality

Rolling out-of-sample volatility performance is assessed using RMSE, MAE, and MAPE.
